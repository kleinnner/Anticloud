# Reducing E-Waste — One App Replaces Multiple Hardware Tokens

## 1. Executive Summary

The electronic waste (e-waste) crisis is one of the most pressing environmental challenges of our time. In 2024, the world generated over 60 million metric tonnes of e-waste, with less than 20% being properly recycled. Hardware security tokens — YubiKeys, RSA SecurIDs, smart cards — contribute to this growing problem.

MF+SO eliminates the need for hardware tokens entirely. This document quantifies the e-waste reduction potential of MF+SO and explains how a software-first approach to authentication can make a meaningful contribution to solving the e-waste crisis.

### 1.1 The Hardware Token E-Waste Problem

| Factor | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| Annual hardware token production | 50-100 million units |
| Average device weight | 10-20 grams |
| Annual e-waste from tokens | 500-2,000 tonnes |
| Recycling rate | < 20% |
| Toxic materials | Lead, brominated flame retardants, PVC |

## 5. Beyond Authentication

### 5.1 Broader E-Waste Context

- Smartphones: 1.5B units/year → 1.5M tonnes e-waste
- Laptops: 250M units/year → 500K tonnes
- Hardware tokens: 100M units/year → 2K tonnes

While hardware tokens are a small fraction of total e-waste, every tonne matters.

### 5.2 Behavioral Impact

- MF+SO users never need to buy a hardware token
- No replacement cycle (every 2-5 years)
- No backup devices (multiple tokens)
- Reduced plastic consumption

## 6. Circular Economy

### 6.1 Software as a Service (Not a Product)

- No physical goods, no waste
- Continuous updates extend life indefinitely
- No replacement cycle
- Zero end-of-life waste

### 6.2 MF+SO E-Waste Policy

- Encourage users to recycle existing hardware tokens
- Provide guidance on electronics recycling
- Advocate for right-to-repair
- Support e-waste legislation
