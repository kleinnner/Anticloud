## 1. Executive Summary

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). It provides a systematic approach to managing sensitive company information, ensuring its confidentiality, integrity, and availability. This document describes MF+SO's ISMS framework, risk assessment methodology, control implementation, and certification roadmap.

MF+SO's security architecture — local-first, zero-knowledge, open source — provides a strong foundation for ISO 27001 compliance. The ISMS covers the relay infrastructure, build pipeline, and organizational processes while accounting for MF+SO's unique architectural characteristics.

### 1.1 ISMS Principles

MF+SO's ISMS is built on the following principles:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Leadership | Top management commitment to information security |
| Risk-based | Controls selected based on risk assessment |
| Continuous improvement | PDCA cycle (Plan-Do-Check-Act) |
| Stakeholder focus | Addressing all stakeholder requirements |
| Process approach | Systematic management of activities |
| Evidence-based | Decisions based on data and analysis |

### 1.2 ISMS Objectives

| Objective | Metric | Target |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| Protect information confidentiality | Data breach incidents | Zero |
| Ensure information integrity | .aioss chain verification failures | < 0.01% |
| Maintain service availability | Relay uptime | 99.9% |
| Achieve ISO 27001 certification | Certification status | Certified |
| Improve security maturity | Maturity assessment score | +15% annually |

## 2. ISO 27001 Overview

### 2.1 Standard Structure

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 consists of:

- **Clauses 4-10**: Mandatory ISMS requirements
- **Annex A**: Reference control objectives and controls (93 controls in 4 domains)

**Clause Structure**:

| Clause | Title | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| 4 | Context of the organization | Understanding the organization and its context |
| 5 | Leadership | Top management commitment and policy |
| 6 | Planning | Risk assessment and risk treatment |
| 7 | Support | Resources, competence, awareness, communication |
| 8 | Operation | Operational planning and control |
| 9 | Performance evaluation | Monitoring, measurement, audit, review |
| 10 | Improvement | Nonconformity, corrective action, continual improvement |

## 5. Risk Assessment Methodology

### 5.1 Risk Assessment Approach

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Methodology | ISO 27005 / NIST SP 800-30 aligned |
| Risk criteria | Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA) |
| Risk levels | Very Low, Low, Medium, High, Very High |
| Assessment frequency | Annual + ad-hoc for major changes |
| Risk owner | Assigned per identified risk |

### 5.2 Risk Identification

Risk sources are identified through:

- Asset identification and valuation
- Threat identification (internal and external)
- Vulnerability identification
- Existing control analysis

**Threat Categories**:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|---------|
| Natural | Earthquake, flood, fire (affecting cloud providers) |
| Human (accidental) | Operator error, misconfiguration, data entry errors |
| Human (deliberate) | Hacking, insider threat, social engineering, malware |
| Technical | Hardware failure, software bugs, network failure |
| Physical | Theft, vandalism, power failure |

### 5.3 Risk Analysis

**Likelihood Rating**:

| Rating | Description | Frequency |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| 1 - Very Low | Highly unlikely | < once per 5 years |
| 2 - Low | Unlikely | 1-2 times per 5 years |
| 3 - Medium | Possible | Once per year |
| 4 - High | Likely | 2-3 times per year |
| 5 - Very High | Almost certain | > 4 times per year |

**Impact Rating**:

| Rating | CIA Impact | Financial | Reputational |
|--------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| 1 - Very Low | Minor data loss | < $10K | Negligible |
| 2 - Low | Limited disclosure | $10K-$50K | Minor |
| 3 - Medium | Moderate breach | $50K-$250K | Moderate |
| 4 - High | Major breach | $250K-$1M | Significant |
| 5 - Very High | Critical compromise | > $1M | Severe |

**Risk Score** = Likelihood × Impact (Maximum: 25)

### 5.4 Risk Evaluation

| Risk Score | Level | Action |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| 1-3 | Very Low | Accept (monitor) |
| 4-6 | Low | Accept or treat with low priority |
| 7-12 | Medium | Treat (plan and implement controls) |
| 13-19 | High | Treat (urgent controls required) |
| 20-25 | Very High | Immediate treatment, consider avoidance |

### 5.5 Risk Register

The risk register documents all identified risks with the following structure:

| Risk ID | Description | Owner | Likelihood | Impact | Score | Treatment | Residual Risk | Review Date |
|---------|-------------|-------|-----------|--------|-------|-----------|---------------|-------------|
