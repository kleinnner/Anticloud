## HEADLINE

# 22-Year-Old Dubai Founder Releases World's First Sovereign AI Operating System

## "The Anti-Cloud" — API-SOS is the first AI that can't be turned off by a foreign government

---

**DUBAI, UAE — [Date]** — Today, API-SOS FZCO announced the release of API-SOS (Agent-Predictive Intelligence Sovereign Operating System), the world's first sovereign AI decision engine — a complete AI operating system that runs entirely on local hardware with zero cloud dependency, zero external API calls, and a cryptographically hash-chained audit trail.

Built by 22-year-old founder Lois Kleinner in Dubai, API-SOS addresses a critical gap in the global AI market: every major AI product — OpenAI's ChatGPT, Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini — depends on US cloud servers under US jurisdiction. Regulated institutions including sovereign wealth funds, central banks, government agencies, hospitals, and law firms cannot legally use these products for sensitive work.

API-SOS solves this by running entirely on hardware under the user's control, with no internet required.

"Every AI company today is selling you a rental," said Lois Kleinner, founder of API-SOS. "They lease you intelligence by the token, route it through their cloud, log it on their servers, and reserve the right to cut you off when a government tells them to. API-SOS exists because rental intelligence is not intelligence — it's dependency dressed as innovation."

**Key features of API-SOS:**
- **Zero cloud dependency** — Runs on any hardware with no internet required
- **.aioss ledger** — SHA-256 hash-chained audit trail, tamper-proof by design
- **Contradiction detection** — 3-layer engine that catches AI hallucinations automatically
- **Multi-agent council** — Risk Analyst, Legal Counsel, and Strategist agents deliberate every output
- **Knowledge graph** — Full provenance tracking with full-text search
- **Document ingestion** — PDF, Excel, DOCX, PPTX, CSV, JSON, HTML
- **Open source** — Apache 2.0 license, publicly auditable on GitHub

API-SOS is currently pursuing validation from the Mohammed Bin Rashid Innovation Fund (MBRIF) for an innovation guarantee.
