# Code of Conduct

## 1. Purpose

The Libern project is committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and
harassment-free environment for everyone, regardless of age, body size,
disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual identity and
orientation, or any other dimension of diversity.

This Code of Conduct outlines our expectations for all those who participate
in our community, as well as the consequences for unacceptable behavior.

## 2. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all Libern community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions, wiki)
- Community chat platforms (Matrix, Discord, forums)
- Social media channels
- In-person events (meetups, conferences, hackathons)
- One-on-one communications related to project business
- Any other venue where community members represent the project

It applies to all community participants, including contributors, maintainers,
Core Team members, Steering Committee members, and guests.

## 3. Our Standards

### 3.1 Expected Behavior

All community participants are expected to:

- **Be welcoming and inclusive:** Make everyone feel welcome, regardless of
  their background or experience level.
- **Be respectful:** Disagreements are inevitable, but they should be handled
  constructively and respectfully. Critique ideas, not people.
- **Be collaborative:** Work together to achieve shared goals. Help others
  learn and grow.
- **Be empathetic:** Consider the perspective of others. What seems obvious to
  you may not be obvious to someone else.
- **Be constructive:** Focus on what is best for the community and the
  project. Provide actionable feedback.
- **Be accountable:** Take responsibility for your actions and their impact.
  If you make a mistake, apologize and learn from it.
- **Prioritize the community:** The health and sustainability of the community
  come before individual interests.

### 3.2 Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are considered unacceptable:

- **Harassment:** Any form of harassment, including but not limited to:
  - Offensive comments related to protected characteristics
  - Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
  - Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
  - Sustained disruption of conversations or events
  - Inappropriate physical contact
  - Publishing private information without consent (doxxing)

- **Discrimination:** Discriminatory language or behavior that excludes,
  demeans, or marginalizes people based on protected characteristics.

- **Violence and threats:** Threats of violence, encouraging violence, or
  violent language directed at another person.

- **Trolling:** Deliberately provocative or inflammatory comments intended to
  disrupt constructive discussion.

- **Personal attacks:** Insults, derogatory comments, or ad hominem attacks.

- **Microaggressions:** Subtle, often unintentional comments or actions that
  communicate bias or prejudice.

- **Sexual content:** Posting or sharing sexual content in community spaces.

- **Spam and self-promotion:** Excessive self-promotion or spam that is not
  relevant to project discussion.

- **Misrepresentation:** Impersonating project members or misrepresenting
  affiliation with the project.

## 4. Enforcement Responsibilities

### 4.1 Core Team

The Core Team is responsible for:
- Investigating reported Code of Conduct violations
- Determining appropriate consequences
- Maintaining confidentiality of reporters
- Documenting enforcement actions

### 4.2 Incident Response Committee

For sensitive or complex cases, the Core Team may form an Incident Response
Committee (IRC) consisting of:
- 3-5 Core Team members
- At least one member from outside the project (if available)
- Representatives from affected communities (if applicable)

### 4.3 Confidentiality

All reports, investigations, and enforcement actions are treated as
confidential to protect the privacy and safety of all parties involved.

## 5. Reporting

### 5.1 How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it:

**Direct report to the Core Team:**
- Email: conduct@libern.ai
- Private message to a Core Team member on community platforms

**Anonymous reporting:**
- Anonymous reporting form: https://libern.ai/conduct/report
- Email using a temporary or anonymous email address

### 5.2 What to Include in a Report

Provide as much information as possible:
- Date and time of the incident
- Location (GitHub issue, chat channel, etc.)
- Description of the behavior
- Who was involved
- Whether the behavior is ongoing
- Any witnesses
- Screenshots or links (if available)
- Your preferred outcome (optional)

### 5.3 Reporting Guidelines

- Reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours
- You will receive an update on the status of your report within 1 week
- We will not disclose your identity without your consent
- Retaliation against reporters is strictly prohibited

## 6. Enforcement Process

### 6.1 Investigation

1. **Initial assessment:** The Core Team assesses the report within 48 hours
2. **Gathering information:** The Core Team interviews relevant parties
3. **Determination:** The Core Team determines whether a violation occurred
4. **Notification:** All parties are notified of the outcome

### 6.2 Factors Considered

When determining consequences, the Core Team considers:
- Severity and frequency of the behavior
- Impact on the affected person(s) and the community
- Whether the behavior was intentional or accidental
- Whether the person has a history of violations
- Whether the person acknowledges and apologizes for the behavior
- The person's role in the community (greater responsibility = greater
  accountability)

### 6.3 Consequences

#### Warning
A private, written warning with an explanation of the violation and expected
behavior going forward. The person may be asked to modify their behavior or
apologize.

#### Temporary Ban
A temporary suspension from all or part of the community (typically 1-30 days).
The length of the ban depends on the severity of the violation.

#### Permanent Ban
Permanent removal from the community. This is reserved for:
- Severe violations (harassment, threats, discrimination)
- Repeated violations after warnings or temporary bans
- Behavior that poses a risk to community safety

#### Other Actions
- Removal from a specific role or responsibility
- Required training or education
- Mediation between parties
- Public acknowledgment of the violation (with consent of affected parties)

### 6.4 Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was unfair, you may appeal:
1. Submit an appeal to the Steering Committee within 14 days
2. The Steering Committee will review the case
3. The Steering Committee's decision is final

Appeals must be based on procedural errors, new evidence, or disproportionate
consequences — not simply disagreement with the decision.

## 7. Community Expectations

### 7.1 Healthy Discussion Guidelines

When participating in project discussions:
- Stay on topic and relevant to the project
- Back up opinions with evidence and reasoning
- Be willing to change your mind when presented with new information
- Disengage from discussions that become unproductive
- Use "I" statements to express your perspective
- Ask clarifying questions before assuming intent

### 7.2 Giving and Receiving Feedback

**Giving feedback:**
- Be specific about what could be improved
- Focus on the work, not the person
- Suggest alternatives, not just problems
- Use constructive language

**Receiving feedback:**
- Do not take feedback personally
- Ask clarifying questions if needed
- Thank the person for their input
- Consider the feedback carefully before responding

### 7.3 Conflict Resolution

When conflicts arise:
1. Try to resolve directly with the other person
2. Involve a neutral third party if needed
3. Escalate to the Core Team if resolution is not possible
4. Focus on the issue, not personalities

## 8. Diversity and Inclusion

The Libern project is committed to:

- **Active inclusion:** We actively work to include people from diverse
  backgrounds and perspectives.
- **Accessible participation:** We strive to make community participation
  accessible to people with disabilities.
- **Global community:** We embrace participants from all countries and
  cultures.
- **Language inclusion:** While English is the primary language, we welcome
  contributions in other languages where possible.

## 9. Moderation

### 9.1 Community Moderation

Moderators have the authority to:
- Remove or edit comments, issues, and posts that violate this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily mute or ban participants from community platforms
- Lock or close discussions that are unproductive or violate conduct standards

### 9.2 Moderation Principles

- Moderation actions are documented and reviewable
- Moderators explain their actions when practical
- Moderation is consistent and fair
- Moderators are accountable to the Core Team

## 18. Restorative Justice Practices

### 18.1 Principles

Restorative justice focuses on repairing harm and restoring community trust:

1. **Acknowledge harm:** The person responsible understands the impact of their actions
2. **Take accountability:** Accept responsibility and commit to change
3. **Make amends:** Take concrete actions to repair the harm
4. **Community healing:** Rebuild trust through consistent positive behavior

### 18.2 Restorative Process

```
Incident Reported
        │
        ▼
Investigation
        │
        ▼
Violation Confirmed?
   ├── No → Case closed, documented
   │
   └── Yes → Restorative process?
        ├── Yes:
        │   ├── Facilitated conversation between affected parties
        │   ├── Harm acknowledged and understood
        │   ├── Action plan developed
        │   ├── Follow-up schedule established
        │   └── Incident documented with outcomes
        │
        └── No (severe/violent/malicious):
            ├── Direct enforcement action (ban)
            └── Incident documented
```

### 18.3 Restorative vs. Punitive Approach

| Aspect | Restorative | Punitive |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Focus | Repairing harm | Punishing violation |
| Question | "What needs to be repaired?" | "What rule was broken?" |
| Outcome | Behavior change + community healing | Isolation of violator |
| Recidivism | Lower (evidence-based) | Higher |
| Community trust | Strengthened | May be damaged |

## 20. Real-World Code of Conduct Scenarios

### 20.1 Scenario: Repeated Disruptive Behavior

**Situation:** A contributor consistently derails technical discussions with off-topic political commentary, despite multiple warnings.

**Resolution path:**
1. Private warning from Core Team (documented)
2. 7-day temporary ban after continued behavior
3. Requirement to review community guidelines before return
4. 30-day observation period with moderated posting
5. Behavior improves → full reinstatement
6. Incident documented for future reference

### 20.2 Scenario: Good-Faith Mistake

**Situation:** A new contributor uses culturally insensitive terminology without realizing it.

**Resolution path:**
1. Community member privately explains why the term is problematic
2. Contributor apologizes and edits their comment
3. Core Team follows up with educational resources
4. No formal action needed — treated as learning opportunity
5. Contributor becomes an advocate for inclusive language


## 13. Commitment

By participating in the Libern community, you agree to abide by this Code of
Conduct. Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our
standards. We expect all community members to help create a safe and welcoming
environment. This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces and also
applies when an individual is representing the project in public spaces.

We prioritize the safety and dignity of marginalized people over the comfort
of privileged people. This means that behavior that makes marginalized
community members feel unsafe, unwelcome, or alienated will not be tolerated.

## 14. Enforcement Examples

### 14.1 Minor Infractions

**Example 1:** A contributor consistently interrupts others during discussions
and dismisses their viewpoints without consideration.

**Response:** A private warning from a Core Team member explaining why the
behavior is problematic and requesting improvement.

**Example 2:** A community member uses mildly inappropriate language in a
public channel.

**Response:** A private reminder of community standards and request to adjust
language.

### 14.2 Moderate Infractions

**Example 1:** A contributor repeatedly makes dismissive or condescending
comments toward newcomers.

**Response:** Temporary ban from community channels (7 days) and a requirement
to review community guidelines before returning.

**Example 2:** A community member posts spam or excessive self-promotion
across multiple channels.

**Response:** Warning and removal of spam content. Repeat offense results in
a 30-day ban.

### 14.3 Severe Infractions

**Example 1:** A contributor makes discriminatory or harassing comments
targeting a protected group.

**Response:** Immediate permanent ban from all community spaces. Report to
platform administrators if appropriate.

**Example 2:** A maintainer abuses their position to suppress contributions
from a competitor or personal rival.

**Response:** Immediate removal from maintainer role and permanent ban from
the project.

**Example 3:** A community member doxxes (publishes private information of)
another community member.

**Response:** Immediate permanent ban. Report to law enforcement if
appropriate.

## 15. Community Moderation Guidelines

### 15.1 Moderation Principles

1. **Transparency:** Moderation actions are explained to affected parties
   whenever possible
2. **Consistency:** Similar violations receive similar consequences
3. **Proportionality:** Consequences match the severity of the violation
4. **Education first:** When possible, use violations as teaching moments
5. **Restorative justice:** Focus on repairing harm and restoring community
   trust

### 15.2 Appeals Process

If you believe a moderation decision was made in error:

1. **Contact the moderator** who took the action (if known) within 14 days
2. **Submit a formal appeal** to the Core Team at conduct-appeals@libern.ai
3. **Include:**
   - The original action and date
   - Why you believe the action was incorrect
   - Any evidence or context that was not considered
4. The Core Team will review and respond within 2 weeks
5. The Steering Committee may hear final appeals

### 15.3 Moderation Transparency Report

The Core Team publishes an annual moderation transparency report including:
- Number of reports received
- Types of violations reported
- Enforcement actions taken
- Appeal outcomes
- Changes to community guidelines or processes
- Lessons learned and improvements made
