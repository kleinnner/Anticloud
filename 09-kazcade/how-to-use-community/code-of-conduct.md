# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the Kazkade community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful**: Value diverse viewpoints and experiences
- **Be constructive**: Focus on what is best for the community
- **Be collaborative**: Work together toward shared goals
- **Be empathetic**: Consider how your words and actions affect others
- **Be professional**: Maintain a professional tone in all interactions
- **Be accountable**: Own your mistakes and learn from them
- **Be inclusive**: Use welcoming language and avoid exclusionary terms

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Unwelcome comments, personal attacks, or trolling
- **Discrimination**: Derogatory comments based on protected characteristics
- **Violence**: Threats of violence or violent language
- **Sexual content**: Unwelcome sexual advances or inappropriate material
- **Doxing**: Publishing others' private information without consent
- **Spam**: Excessive self-promotion or irrelevant content
- **Disruption**: Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- **Misinformation**: Knowingly spreading false information

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### Enforcement Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| **Moderators** | Handle day-to-day moderation in Discord/Matrix |
| **Maintainers** | Escalate issues in GitHub Issues/PRs |
| **Steering Committee** | Final authority on enforcement decisions |

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories (issues, PRs, discussions, wiki)
- Discord server and Matrix channel
- Community forums and mailing lists
- In-person events (meetups, conferences)
- Official social media channels
- Private correspondence when representing the community

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, report it:

- **Email**: conduct@kazcade.io
- **GitHub**: Create a private issue labeled `Moderation`
- **Discord**: DM any moderator

Reports are treated with confidentiality. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### What to Include

When reporting, please include:

1. **Contact info**: Your name and preferred contact method
2. **Description**: What happened, when, and where
3. **Context**: Any relevant screenshots, logs, or links
4. **Impact**: How the behavior affected you or the community
5. **Witnesses**: Anyone else who observed the incident

### Confidentiality

- Reports are kept confidential to the extent possible
- Only the enforcement team has access to reports
- Your identity will not be shared without your consent

## Enforcement Process

### 1. Warning

A private, written warning from community leaders with clarity about the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate.

### 2. Temporary Ban

A temporary ban from any community interaction (including GitHub, Discord, and events) for a specified period of time (typically 1-4 weeks).

### 3. Permanent Ban

A permanent ban from any community interaction for repeated violations, severe harassment, or threats.

### Appeal Process

If you believe an enforcement action was unfair:

1. Email `appeals@kazcade.io` within 14 days
2. Include the original case number and your reasoning
3. The Steering Committee will review within 7 days

## Community Standards for Technical Contributions

### Code Review Conduct

- Focus on the code, not the person
- Explain the "why" behind suggestions
- Accept feedback gracefully
- Distinguish between "must fix" and "nice to have"

### Communication Channels

| Channel | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| GitHub Issues | Bug reports, feature requests |
| GitHub Discussions | Questions, ideas, discussions |
| Discord | Real-time chat, quick questions |
| GitHub PRs | Code review |

### Language

Use English for all community communication to ensure everyone can participate.
