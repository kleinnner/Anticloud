# ΔaaS — Delta as a Service

**Δ is not change. Δ is the operator that keeps your system in superposition.**

[![Zenodo](https://img.shields.io/badge/Research-Zenodo-1682D4?style=flat-square&logo=zenodo)](https://zenodo.org/search?q=anticloud)
[![Harvard Dataverse](https://img.shields.io/badge/Data-Harvard%20Dataverse-8B4513?style=flat-square&logo=dataverse)](https://dataverse.harvard.edu/dataverse/anticloud)

Every "as a Service" you have ever used collapses you into a fixed state. You are on AWS, Azure, or GCP. You are a tenant, not a sovereign. Your data lives on their hardware, under their jurisdiction, subject to their terms.

ΔaaS is the first service that does not collapse you.

The Δ operator — borrowed from quantum mechanics — represents a system in superposition. A quantum delta state is not one thing or another; it is all possibilities simultaneously until observed. ΔaaS applies this principle to computing infrastructure: your system exists in a superposition of all possible providers, architectures, and paradigms until you choose to collapse it.

**ΔaaS is not hosted. It is deployed.** One binary, one machine, zero trust in anyone. The "service" in ΔaaS is ironic by design — it means the capability is delivered to you as a deployable unit, not as a dependency on someone else's infrastructure.

### Core Principle

```
Δ|ψ⟩ = |sovereign⟩ + |cloud⟩
```

Your system exists in superposition. You collapse it when — and only when — you choose to.

### In the Anticloud Ecosystem

ΔaaS is the philosophical foundation of the Anticloud. Every project — Kathon, API-OSS, Kamelot, MF+SO, Kasteran, Inte11ect — is a realization of the Δ principle: sovereign, local-first infrastructure that exists without collapsing into cloud dependency.
