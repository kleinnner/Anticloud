## Response Time SLAs

### Severity Definitions

| Severity | Definition | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| **S1 — Critical** | System down, data at risk, core functionality unavailable | Daemon crash on startup, data corruption, search non-functional |
| **S2 — High** | Major feature degraded, significant performance impact | Search >5s, indexing stalled, FUSE mount issues |
| **S3 — Medium** | Non-critical feature impaired, minor performance impact | Thumbnail previews not working, slow but usable search |
| **S4 — Low** | Cosmetic issues, documentation errors, feature requests | UI color issue, documentation typo, configuration enhancement |

### Response Time Targets

| Severity | Bronze | Silver | Gold | Platinum |
|----------|--------|--------|------|----------|
| S1 | 4 hours | 2 hours | 1 hour | 30 minutes |
| S2 | 8 hours | 4 hours | 2 hours | 1 hour |
| S3 | 24 hours | 12 hours | 8 hours | 4 hours |
| S4 | 48 hours | 24 hours | 16 hours | 8 hours |

### Response Time Definition

"Response time" means:
- **Initial acknowledgment** from a support engineer
- Not necessarily resolution
- Resolution target is within the same time window for S1-S2
- Resolution target is within 2x response time for S3-S4

### Escalation

Escalation path for unresolved issues:
1. **Tier 1**: Support engineer (acknowledgment within SLA)
2. **Tier 2**: Senior engineer (if not resolved within 2x SLA)
3. **Tier 3**: Engineering lead (if critical or unresolved within 24 hours)
4. **Tier 4**: CTO / VP Engineering (executive escalation)

### SLA Credits

If response time SLAs are missed, customers may be eligible for service credits:

| Missed SLA | Credit |
|-----------|--------|
| 1st occurrence in quarter | 5% of monthly fee |
| 2nd occurrence in quarter | 10% of monthly fee |

### Creating a Support Ticket

1. Log in to [support.kamelot.ai](https://support.kamelot.ai)
2. Click "New Ticket"
3. Select severity (S1-S4) — be accurate to ensure appropriate response
4. Fill in the ticket template:
   - Summary (1-2 sentences)
   - Environment details (OS, Kamelot version, hardware)
   - Steps to reproduce
   - Expected vs actual behavior
   - Logs and screenshots (attached)
5. Submit
6. You'll receive an automated acknowledgment with a ticket number

### Ticket Lifecycle

1. **Submitted**: Ticket received, automated acknowledgment sent
2. **Triaged**: Severity verified, assigned to appropriate engineer
3. **In Progress**: Engineer working on the issue
4. **Pending Customer**: Waiting for additional information
5. **Resolved**: Solution provided, awaiting confirmation
6. **Closed**: Confirmed resolved (or auto-closed after 14 days of no response)

## Support Processes

### Incident Management Process

```
User submits ticket
    ↓
Automated acknowledgment (within 5 minutes)
    ↓
Triage (within SLA time)
    ↓
  ├── Severity confirmed
  ├── Engineer assigned
  └── Initial diagnosis
    ↓
Resolution work
    ↓
  ├── Fix implemented (if applicable)
  ├── Workaround documented
  └── Root cause identified
    ↓
User verification
    ↓
  ├── User confirms resolution
  └── or → Return to resolution work
    ↓
Ticket closed
    ↓
Post-incident review (S1 and S2 only)
    ↓
  ├── Root cause analysis document
  ├── Preventative measures
  └── Knowledge base article (if applicable)
```

### Severity Classification Guidelines

**S1 — Critical:**
- Complete loss of search functionality
- Data corruption or loss
- Daemon crash on startup
- Security breach
- Response: Immediate, continuous effort until resolved

**S2 — High:**
- Search latency >10 seconds
- Indexing stalled for >4 hours
- FUSE mount unavailable
- Feature unusable for >25% of users
- Response: Continuous effort during business hours

**S3 — Medium:**
- Minor feature degradation
- Performance below baseline but usable
- UI issues (non-critical)
- Documentation errors
- Response: Best-effort during business hours

**S4 — Low:**
- Cosmetic issues
- Feature requests
- Documentation suggestions
- General inquiries
- Response: Next business day

### Escalation Matrix

| Level | Person | Trigger | Response Time |
|-------|--------|---------|---------------|
| L1 | Support Engineer | Ticket assigned | Within SLA |
| L2 | Senior Support Engineer | Not resolved in 2x SLA | 1 hour |
| L3 | Engineering Lead | Critical issue, >4 hours | 30 minutes |
| L4 | CTO / VP Engineering | Executive request, >8 hours | 15 minutes |
