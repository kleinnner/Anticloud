# Kasteran* — ISO 27001 Compliance
© Lois-Kleinner & 0-1.gg 2026

## Overview

ISO 27001 is the international standard for information security management systems (ISMS). Kasteran* has been developed with full alignment to the ISO 27001 framework, ensuring that organizations using the language can achieve and maintain certification with minimal additional effort. This document outlines how Kasteran* supports each clause of the ISO 27001 standard.

## Information Security Management System (ISMS)

Kasteran* provides the technical foundation for an ISMS by embedding security controls into the language runtime and compiler. The ISMS context requires understanding the organization and its environment. Kasteran* supports this by providing comprehensive audit trails, asset inventories through the dependency management system, and stakeholder identification through the community governance framework.

### Leadership and Commitment

Top management commitment is demonstrated through the Kasteran* governance model, which includes defined roles, responsibilities, and accountability for information security. The core team maintains an information security policy that applies to all contributors and users. This policy is reviewed at regular intervals and is publicly accessible.

### Planning

Kasteran* supports risk assessment and treatment planning through its security features. The type system eliminates many common risks at compile time. For risks that cannot be eliminated, the language provides mitigation mechanisms such as encryption, access control, and audit logging. Risk treatment plans can be codified as compiler policies.

### Support

The Kasteran* project provides extensive documentation, training materials, and community support to ensure that all personnel involved in the development and operation of Kasteran*-based systems are competent. Awareness programs are conducted through webinars, documentation, and community events. Communication channels are defined for security-related matters.

## Risk Management

Risk management is central to ISO 27001. Kasteran* supports risk identification, analysis, and evaluation through its telemetry and monitoring systems. The deterministic build system ensures that risks introduced through the supply chain are minimized. Dependency scanning identifies vulnerabilities in third-party libraries automatically.

### Risk Assessment Methodology

Kasteran* follows a quantitative and qualitative risk assessment methodology. The compiler produces security reports that identify potential vulnerabilities based on code analysis. These reports include severity ratings, likelihood assessments, and recommended mitigations. The methodology is documented and reproducible.

### Risk Treatment

Risk treatment options include risk avoidance (eliminated by the type system), risk reduction (through encryption and access controls), risk transfer (through insurance and third-party agreements), and risk acceptance (documented and approved). Kasteran* provides tooling for each treatment option, including automated policy enforcement.

## Security Controls (Annex A)

Kasteran* maps to all relevant Annex A controls:

### Information Security Policies (A.5)
The Kasteran* project maintains a security policy that is reviewed annually and communicated to all stakeholders.

### Organization of Information Security (A.6)
Roles and responsibilities for information security are defined in the governance model. Segregation of duties is enforced through the access control system.

### Human Resource Security (A.7)
Background checks are conducted for core team members. Security training is provided through documentation and community resources.

### Asset Management (A.8)
All code, documentation, and infrastructure assets are inventoried and classified. The dependency management system provides a software bill of materials (SBOM) for every project.

### Access Control (A.9)
Kasteran* implements role-based access control (RBAC) and attribute-based access control (ABAC) with the principle of least privilege. Access reviews are conducted quarterly.

### Cryptography (A.10)
AES-256, TLS 1.3, and zero-knowledge proofs are supported natively. Key management follows NIST standards.

### Physical and Environmental Security (A.11)
While language-level controls cannot address physical security, the Kasteran* deployment infrastructure (CI/CD, repositories) is hosted in SOC 2-compliant data centers with physical access controls.

### Operations Security (A.12)
Procedures are documented, change management is enforced, capacity management is automated, and malware protection is integrated into the build pipeline.

### Communications Security (A.13)
Network security is enforced through the runtime, with support for TLS, mTLS, and network policies.

### System Acquisition, Development, and Maintenance (A.14)
Security requirements are specified for all development work. The compiler enforces secure coding standards. Testing includes security testing in the CI pipeline.

### Supplier Relationships (A.15)
Third-party dependencies are vetted and monitored. Supplier agreements include security requirements.

### Incident Management (A.16)
The breach response process is documented and tested. Incidents are reported, analyzed, contained, and remediated.

### Business Continuity (A.17)
Kasteran* applications support high-availability deployments with circuit breaker patterns, failover, and disaster recovery.

### Compliance (A.18)
Compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual requirements is documented and monitored. This document is part of that compliance evidence.

## Continuous Improvement

ISO 27001 requires continuous improvement of the ISMS. Kasteran* supports this through regular security assessments, vulnerability scanning, and community feedback loops. Non-conformities are tracked and corrective actions are implemented. The language evolves based on security findings and emerging threats.

## Internal Audit

Kasteran* undergoes regular internal audits of its security controls. The audit logging system provides the evidence needed for these audits. Audit findings are tracked to closure. The audit program is risk-based, focusing on high-risk areas.

## Management Review

The Kasteran* core team conducts management reviews at planned intervals. These reviews assess the ISMS performance, security metrics, audit results, and stakeholder feedback. Decisions and actions are documented and communicated.

## Conclusion

Kasteran* provides comprehensive support for ISO 27001 compliance. Organizations using Kasteran* can leverage the language's built-in security features to accelerate their certification process and maintain ongoing compliance with reduced operational overhead.
