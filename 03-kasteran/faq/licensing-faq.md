# Kasteran* — Licensing FAQ
© Lois-Kleinner & 0-1.gg 2026

## What license is Kasteran* under?

Kasteran* is released under the MIT License — one of the most permissive open source licenses. You can find the full license text in the LICENSE file in the repository.

## Can I use Kasteran* commercially?

Yes. The MIT license permits commercial use without any restrictions or fees.

## Can I distribute Kasteran* with my product?

Yes. You can include Kasteran* binaries in your product distribution. The MIT license requires that you include the copyright notice and permission notice.

## Do I need to open-source my code?

No. The MIT license does not require you to open-source your code. You can keep your source code proprietary even if you use Kasteran*.

## What about the patent grant?

The MIT license does not include an explicit patent grant. However, by distributing Kasteran*, we are impliedly granting any patents necessary to use the code. If patent concerns are significant, consider dual-licensing or additional patent agreements.

## Can I create a fork?

Yes. The MIT license permits forking and creating derivative works. You can modify Kasteran* and distribute your modifications under the same or different terms, as long as you include the original copyright notice.

## Can I relicense Kasteran*?

You can relicense your modifications, but the original Kasteran* code must remain under the MIT license. Any derivative work must include the original copyright notice.

## What about dependencies?

Dependencies may have different licenses:
- **MIT**: Compatible
- **Apache 2.0**: Compatible
- **BSD**: Compatible
- **LGPL**: Compatible (if dynamically linked)
- **GPL**: Consider compatibility requirements

Use `kasteran audit licenses` to check dependency licenses.

## Do contributors retain copyright?

Yes. Contributors retain copyright of their contributions but grant the project the right to distribute their contributions under the MIT license.

## How is attribution handled?

The MIT license requires attribution. Kasteran* includes:
- LICENSE file in the repository
- Copyright notice in source files
- Third-party notices for dependencies
- Automatic attribution generation for your projects

## Can I get a commercial license?

The MIT license already permits commercial use. If you need additional terms (e.g., patent protection, indemnification), please contact licensing@kasteran.dev.

## Conclusion

Kasteran* MIT license provides maximum freedom for users and contributors. You can use, modify, and distribute the language freely for any purpose, including commercial applications.
